Executive Summary

The Shopify store is the brand’s owned digital storefront — the only channel where the brand controls the entire customer experience: the visual design, the messaging, the pricing, the checkout flow, and the post-purchase relationship. Amazon is the volume channel (search traffic, Prime shipping, review velocity). Shopify is the BRAND channel (storytelling, email capture, higher margin, B2B inquiry). Both are essential. Shopify builds the brand; Amazon builds the revenue.

This document specifies every element of the Shopify store: an 8-section sustainability-led homepage designed to communicate the value proposition in 3 seconds, product listings with 7–9 image galleries and SEO-optimized titles, an auto-updating impact counter showing garbage bags replaced and waste composted, payments with express checkout (Shop Pay, Apple Pay), free shipping at $25+ to drive multi-pack AOV, Klaviyo email automation with 5 flow types (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase, pre-race, sunset), a dedicated B2B landing page for race director inquiries, and a mobile optimization checklist for the 70%+ of runners who browse on phone.

1. Homepage: 8-Section Blueprint

The homepage is the brand’s digital storefront. A visitor arriving from Google, Instagram, or a QR code has 3 seconds to understand: WHAT the product is, WHO it’s for, and WHY it matters. The 8 sections below flow in a deliberate sequence: attention (hero) → education (how it works) → trust (social proof) → impact (counter) → conversion (product showcase) → credibility (race logos) → connection (brand story) → capture (email signup).

Section Content Notes
Hero section Full-width hero image: runner in poncho at dawn, start-line atmosphere, golden light, metallized lining catching the sun. Headline: “Race Warm. Leave No Trace.” (or winning tagline from P1A/P1C). Subheadline: “The first BPI-certified compostable warmth poncho for marathon start lines.” CTA button: “Shop Now” (links to product page). Secondary CTA: “Race Directors: Get a Free Sample Kit” (links to B2B landing page, P6B Section 6). ABOVE THE FOLD. The hero image and headline must communicate the ENTIRE value proposition in 3 seconds: warmth + sustainability + runners. Test: if someone screenshots only the top of the homepage, can they understand what the product is? If yes, the hero is working.

The 3-step simplicity addresses the “what do I do with it?” question that every new visitor has.

Target: 3–6% of homepage visitors sign up (per P6A Section 6 KPI targets).

1.1 Homepage Design Principles

MINIMAL TEXT: runners scan, they don’t read. Every section should communicate its message in under 5 seconds of visual scanning. STRONG IMAGERY: the hero image, product photos, and race-day photography do the heavy lifting. Text supports the images, not the other way around. SINGLE CTA PER SECTION: each section has one clear action (shop, sign up, learn more, inquire). Multiple CTAs per section create choice paralysis. BRAND CONSISTENCY: all design elements follow P1B visual identity: Earth Green (#2D6A4F), Dawn Gold (#D4A843), Warm Charcoal (#2B2D2F), Mist White (#F5F2EB). Typography: DM Sans headings, Inter body. Imagery: warm dawn tones per P1D photography direction.

2.1 Product Page Anatomy

Every element on the product page serves a conversion function. The page is structured in a vertical hierarchy that mirrors the buyer’s decision process: see it (images), want it (headline + bullets), trust it (reviews + certifications), and buy it (Add to Cart + express pay).

Element Specification

First image must be the hero shot on white background — this is the thumbnail in search results and collection pages.
Title Format: “[BRAND] Compostable Warmth Poncho — [Pack Size] | BPI Certified | Race Start Line”. Example: “[BRAND] Compostable Warmth Poncho — 3-Pack | BPI Certified | Race Start Line.” Include: brand name, product type, pack size, and primary selling point in the title for SEO.

Display: star rating, total review count, and individual reviews with reviewer name, date, rating, and text. Photo reviews are 3–5× more persuasive than text-only — incentivize with a “Leave a photo review, get 15% off your next order” offer.
Related products Below reviews: “Complete Your Race Kit” section showing the other pack sizes (if viewing single: show 3-pack and 6-pack as upsells). Also: “Race directors: Order for your start line” link to the B2B landing page.

3. Impact Counter: Real-Time Sustainability Proof

The impact counter is the homepage’s emotional anchor. It transforms the brand from a product company into a MOVEMENT. Visitors don’t just buy a poncho — they contribute to a growing counter of garbage bags replaced, pounds composted, and races served. The counter provides: social proof (others are buying and making an impact), momentum (the numbers grow visibly over time), and accountability (the brand publishes its impact transparently).

Element Specification Notes
Data source Google Sheet with 3 columns: total_garbage_bags_replaced (= total units sold × 1, since each poncho replaces 1 garbage bag), total_lbs_composted (= total units sold to B2B × 0.06 lbs/poncho × collection rate), total_races_served (= count of distinct races that have used the product). Update: weekly from sales data (Shopify + Amazon + B2B). Manual update is acceptable for Season 1. For Season 2+: automate via Shopify webhook → Google Sheets API or a custom app.
Frontend display Large numeric counters with animated count-up effect on page load (e.g., 0 → 12,450 over 2 seconds). Three metrics side by side: [X] GARBAGE BAGS REPLACED | [Y] LBS COMPOSTED | [Z] RACES SERVED. Background: Earth Green band across full page width. Typography: large DM Sans bold numbers, small Inter label text below. Count-up animation is a simple JavaScript effect: many Shopify themes include it natively (“counter section” or “stats section”). If theme doesn’t include: use a free Shopify app (e.g., “CountUp — Animated Counters”) or add custom Liquid/JavaScript to the theme.
Implementation options OPTION A (simplest): Use a Shopify theme section with manually updated values. Edit the theme → add a “Counter” or “Stats” section to the homepage → enter the 3 numbers. Update weekly. OPTION B (semi-automated): Embed a Google Sheet chart or data via iframe. The Sheet updates when sales data is entered; the homepage reflects changes automatically. OPTION C (fully automated, Year 2+): Custom Shopify app that pulls Shopify order count and calculates impact metrics in real time. Displays dynamically on the homepage. RECOMMENDED: Option A for launch. Takes 10 minutes to set up, 2 minutes to update weekly. The manual update cadence matches the Monday KPI dashboard review (P6A Section 6).
Seeding the counter Pre-launch: if pilot race data exists (P5B), seed the counter with real numbers from pilot races (“2,500 garbage bags replaced, 150 lbs composted, 3 races served”). If no pilot data yet: start at 0 and let the counter grow from actual sales/races. Do NOT fabricate numbers — the counter must reflect real, verifiable impact. Transparency is the brand’s foundation. Starting at 0 is fine and even powerful: visitors who return later see the counter growing, which creates a sense of momentum and community participation.

4. Payments, Shipping, Tax & Policies

Frictionless payments and transparent shipping are the foundation of DTC conversion. A 2.9% processing fee on a $9.99 sale costs $0.59 — trivial compared to the $9.99 lost from a customer who abandons because PayPal isn’t accepted or shipping costs are hidden until checkout. Show everything upfront, accept everything, and surprise with nothing.

Element Specification
Payment processor Shopify Payments (built-in, recommended). Processing fee: 2.9% + $0.30 per online transaction (Basic plan). Supports: credit/debit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay. NO additional transaction fee when using Shopify Payments. Alternative: Stripe (same rates) or PayPal (2.99% + $0.49). RECOMMENDATION: enable Shopify Payments + PayPal as a secondary option (some customers prefer PayPal).
Express checkout Enable: Shop Pay (auto-saves customer info for 1-click checkout — conversion rate increase of 1.7× per Shopify data), Apple Pay, Google Pay. All are enabled in Shopify Payments settings. These appear on the product page as dynamic checkout buttons and in the cart as express checkout options.
Shipping rates FREE SHIPPING on orders ≥25 (incentivizes 3-pack purchase at $24.99). Below $25: flat rate $4.99. Ship via: USPS First Class (2–5 days for packages <13 oz — the poncho qualifies) or USPS Priority Mail (2–3 days for larger orders). Use Pirate Ship (pirateship.com) for discounted USPS rates (40–70% off retail). Shopify Shipping (discounted UPS/USPS rates available directly in Shopify) is an alternative.
Shipping zones US domestic only for Season 1 (continental US). Add: Alaska, Hawaii at +$3 surcharge (or free shipping threshold of $35). International shipping: NOT recommended for Season 1 (customs complexity, higher shipping cost, longer transit = higher return rate). Add in Year 2 if demand warrants (start with Canada, then UK/EU).
Tax collection Enable Shopify’s automatic tax calculation: Settings → Taxes and Duties → United States → enable automatic tax calculation. Shopify uses Avalara’s tax engine to calculate the correct state + city + county tax rate based on the customer’s shipping address. For states with economic nexus thresholds: monitor total sales per state. If any state exceeds the nexus threshold (typically $100K in sales or 200 transactions): register for sales tax in that state. Use TaxJar ($19–$99/month) for automated multi-state filing when volume warrants.
Store policies Create 4 policy pages in Shopify Admin → Settings → Policies: (1) Shipping Policy: delivery timelines, free shipping threshold, carrier information. (2) Return Policy: per P4B Section 6.1 (30-day, unused/unopened, full refund). (3) Privacy Policy: disclose GA4, Meta Pixel, Clarity, cookies, email collection. Use Shopify’s built-in privacy policy generator + customize for tracking tools. (4) Terms of Service: standard Shopify-generated template. All 4 policies are linked in the site footer and are legally required for e-commerce.

5. Klaviyo Email Automation: 5 Flow Types

Email marketing generates $36–$42 for every $1 spent (DMA industry data) — the highest ROI of any marketing channel. Klaviyo integrates natively with Shopify and enables automated email flows that trigger based on customer behavior: sign up, abandon cart, purchase, inactivity. These flows run 24/7 without founder intervention, generating revenue while the founder sleeps.

Flow Specification
Setup Install Klaviyo Shopify app (free up to 250 contacts, then $20–$45/month). Connect to Shopify: Klaviyo automatically syncs: customer data, order history, product catalog, and browsing behavior. Enable: on-site tracking (Klaviyo’s JavaScript snippet — installed automatically by the app).
Welcome series (3 emails) Trigger: email sign-up (popup, inline form, or checkout). Email 1 (immediate): “Welcome to [BRAND]! Here’s your 10% off code: WELCOME10. Use it on your first order.” Include: hero image, 3-step product overview, CTA to shop. Email 2 (Day 3): “Why We Built This” — the brand story from P1C Section 1.2. Emotional connection, founder photo, mission statement. CTA: “Shop Our Story.” Email 3 (Day 7): “Your 10% Off Expires Soon” — urgency reminder with product images and CTA. Include: testimonial quote from a pilot race runner.
Abandoned cart (3 emails) Trigger: item added to cart, checkout initiated, but purchase not completed. Email 1 (1 hour after abandonment): “You left something behind!” Product image, price, “Complete Your Order” CTA. No discount yet. Email 2 (24 hours): “Still thinking about it?” Add: 1–2 testimonial quotes, FAQ answer (“Yes, it really keeps you warm”). CTA: “Complete Your Order.” Email 3 (72 hours): “Last chance + free shipping.” Offer: free shipping on the abandoned order (code: FREESHIP or automatic). This is the conversion trigger.
Post-purchase (4 emails) Trigger: purchase completed. Email 1 (immediate): order confirmation (Shopify handles this natively, but Klaviyo can customize with branded design). Email 2 (Day 3 after delivery): “How’s your poncho?” Quick satisfaction check + composting instructions. CTA: “Learn how to compost your poncho.” Email 3 (Day 7): “Leave a Review” request. “Tell other runners what you think! Leave a review and get 15% off your next order.” Direct link to Shopify review page (Judge.me or Loox). Email 4 (Day 14): “Share the Warmth” referral prompt per P1E Section 6: “Give $5, Get $5. Share your referral link: brand.com/refer/[unique-code].”
Pre-race flow Trigger: geographic/behavioral. If a major race is 2–4 weeks away AND the subscriber’s location is the race city (or they’ve browsed race-specific content): send a “Don’t Forget Your Poncho” email. “[Race Name] is [X] days away. Race-morning forecast: [temp]. Order your compostable poncho now and it’ll arrive before race day.” Include: shipping guarantee (“Order by [date] for delivery before [race name]”).
Sunset / re-engagement Trigger: subscriber has not opened an email in 90 days. Email 1 (90 days): “We miss you! Here’s 15% off your next poncho.” Email 2 (120 days): “Last chance — should we keep you on our list?” If no engagement after Email 2: suppress from active list (stop sending to protect deliverability). Re-add if they visit the site or make a purchase.

5.1 Email Design Guidelines

ALL Klaviyo emails must follow P1B visual identity: header with brand logo on Earth Green background, body on Mist White, CTA buttons in Earth Green with white text, footer with social links + unsubscribe. Mobile-first design: 600px max width, single-column layout, 14px+ body text, 44px+ tap targets for buttons. Keep emails under 500 words — runners are busy. Every email has ONE primary CTA (not 3 competing links). Subject lines: <50 characters, personalized with first name, no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation.

7. Mobile Optimization: 70%+ Traffic

More than 70% of running-related online shopping occurs on mobile devices. The Shopify store must be designed MOBILE-FIRST: every element, from the hero image to the checkout button, must work perfectly on a 375px-wide screen with a thumb as the input device. Desktop is the secondary experience.

Element Specification
Theme selection Choose a Shopify theme optimized for mobile: Dawn (free, Shopify’s default, fast, clean), Prestige ($350, premium, beautiful on mobile), or Impact ($350, bold, high-conversion). ALL Shopify 2.0 themes are responsive, but some render better on mobile than others. TEST: open the store on 3 different phones (iPhone, Samsung, budget Android) and verify: images load quickly, text is readable without zooming, buttons are tappable without misclicks, and checkout flows smoothly.
Navigation Hamburger menu (standard mobile navigation) with: Shop (links to product collection), Our Story (brand.com/about), Race Directors (brand.com/b2b), Composting (brand.com/compost), FAQ, and Cart icon with item count badge. Keep navigation to 5–7 items maximum. Every extra item reduces clarity.
Product page mobile layout Image gallery: swipeable carousel (standard in all Shopify themes). Price + Add to Cart: visible IMMEDIATELY below the first image without scrolling. Express checkout buttons (Shop Pay, Apple Pay): directly below Add to Cart. Description bullets: collapsible accordion (tap to expand) to save screen space. Reviews: displayed below description with star rating visible at the top of the product page (near the title) as a clickable link that scrolls to the reviews section.
Page speed Target: <3 second full page load on mobile (4G connection). Optimize: compress all product images (TinyPNG or Shopify’s built-in compression), minimize third-party apps (each app adds JavaScript that slows the page — only install essential apps), use system fonts where possible (DM Sans + Inter may require Google Fonts loading; if page speed suffers, fall back to Arial/Helvetica). Test: Google PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev) → target: 70+ mobile performance score.
Checkout optimization Shopify’s checkout is already mobile-optimized, but verify: (1) Guest checkout is enabled (Settings → Checkout → Customer accounts: “Accounts are optional”). Requiring account creation kills conversion — 30–40% of users abandon checkout if forced to create an account. (2) Address autocomplete is enabled (Shopify uses Google Places API by default). (3) Express checkout buttons are enabled and visible. (4) Order summary is collapsible on mobile (reduces vertical scroll in checkout).
Testing cadence Before launch: test the entire purchase flow on mobile (browse → product page → add to cart → checkout → purchase) on at least 3 devices. Monthly: spot-check the mobile experience after any theme update, app installation, or product page change. Use Clarity session recordings filtered by “mobile” to identify mobile-specific friction points.

9. Conclusion: The Store Is the Brand Experience

A runner who visits brand.com should FEEL the brand before they buy the product. The hero image should evoke the cold dawn of a race morning. The impact counter should make them feel part of something bigger. The tear-away demo photos should make them curious. The race director quote should make them trust. And the “Add to Cart” button should feel like the obvious next step, not a hard sell.

Shopify is not just an online store — it is the brand’s home base. Amazon is a marketplace (the brand is one of many). The expo is temporary (2 days, then gone). Social media is rented (algorithms control reach). But brand.com is OWNED. The email list is OWNED. The customer relationship is OWNED. Every Shopify visitor who signs up for email becomes a direct relationship the brand controls forever — no algorithm, no marketplace fee, no platform risk. Build the store to sell ponchos. Build the email list to build a business.

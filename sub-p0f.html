Executive Summary

Landed COGS of $1.34–$2.48/unit (100K–5K volumes) enables $9.99 DTC retail and $1.75–$2.50 B2B wholesale with 55–75% gross margins. The $9.99 Amazon price is structurally optimal: Low-Price FBA saves $0.86/unit and Clothing referral drops to 8% (vs. 15% above $10). Registration add-ons deliver the highest-margin channel at 56–60% net ($6.07/unit). Year 1 profitability requires ~25K units at $35–50K investment.

REVISION NOTE (April 2026): This P0F document was completed during the market research phase before final product specification and pricing decisions were made in P2–P8. B2B pricing in this document ($1.75–$2.50/unit) was revised upward to $3.50–$5.00/unit during P5A pipeline development based on: BPI certification value, composting logistics inclusion, and competitive positioning. Crossing $10 costs $1.56+/unit extra. $9.99 is mathematically optimal. Products ≤$10 qualify for Low-Price FBA ($0.86 savings) and 8% Clothing referral (vs. 15%). Amazon PPC: $0.30–$1.00 CPC; launch ACoS 30–50%; budget $500–$1K/month for 3 months.

4. Expo Sales Economics

Major expo booths: $5K–$8K rental; all-in $9.3K–$19.1K (NYC/CHI/BOS). Mid-size: $2.1K–$6.5K all-in. NYC expo: 100K+ visitors, 79% purchase rate, $86 median spend.

Selling 750 units at major expo with $12K costs = ~$4,500 loss. Mid-size expo selling 200 units at $2.5K costs = ~$134 profit. Expos are marketing investments: brand awareness, runner feedback, email capture, and race-director networking offset thin/negative direct margins.

5. Registration Add-Ons: Highest-Margin Channel

RunSignUp: 6% + $1/cart. Effective fee: ~$0.60–$0.72/unit. No subscription or setup fees. 94% of races pass fees to participants. ACTIVE.com: ~$0.99 + 5.9%.

Model: 10% of 50K registrants add $9.99 poncho = 5,000 units. $49,950 revenue − $3,600 fees − $10,000 COGS − $6,000 fulfillment = $30,350 net profit (60.8% margin). Per-unit: $6.07 net. Packet-pickup fulfillment: $1.00–$1.20/unit. Scales across unlimited races with near-zero fixed costs.

6. Multi-Pack & Subscription Economics

432K US marathon finishers (5% YoY growth); 2.1M half-marathon finishers. Average runner: $1,748/year spend; only 17% repeat same event. Recommended: 3-pack $24.99 (17% discount), 6-pack $44.99 (25% discount), Subscribe & Save 15% + free shipping.

CAC: $50–$90 paid social; lower with micro-influencers ($200–$800/post). 78.8% of DTC customers make only one purchase; subscriber LTV is 2.7× non-subscriber. LTV:CAC target 3:1 requires $150–$270 LTV — achievable through bundles and subscriptions only.

9. Price Sensitivity Summary

Van Westendorp estimates: too cheap <$3.99, bargain $5.99–$7.99, optimal $8.99–$9.99, expensive $12.99–$14.99, too expensive >$17.99. Eco premium: consumers state 9.7–12% willingness (PwC/Bain); actual 1–5% for 30% of buyers. Marathon demographics (HHI >$100K) skew higher.

By channel: Amazon $9.99 (Low-Price FBA + impulse), expo $9.99–$12.99 (median $86 spend), registration add-on $7.99–$9.99 (marginal vs. $100–$300 entry), B2B $1.50–$2.50 (within warmth budget).

11. Strategic Pricing Recommendations

1. Anchor at $9.99 DTC. Structural optimization: Low-Price FBA, 8% referral, impulse range, marginal alongside race registration.

2. B2B at $1.75–$2.50 with volume tiers. Fits warmth budget; justified by sustainability for CRS/Evergreen-seeking races.

3. Prioritize registration add-ons. $6.07/unit net vs. $1.13 Amazon. Near-zero fixed costs. Target 10–15 race partnerships Year 1.

4. Bundle for DTC. 3-pack $24.99 free shipping. Subscribe & Save 15% discount. Single-unit DTC is margin-negative.

5. Begin certification immediately. BPI (12–18 months, $15–$45K) is longest lead. Leverage NatureFlex NM existing certs. Target soft launch Fall 2026.

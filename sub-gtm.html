1. Executive Summary

This gap analysis evaluates ALL 60 subproject documents (P0A–P10F) and the GTM Master Tracker against: (A) task requirement coverage (does the document address every task assigned in the tracker?), (B) cross-reference consistency (do figures, specifications, and strategies align across documents?), (C) tracker accuracy (does the tracker reflect the actual completion status and current specifications?), and (D) content quality (is the depth and specificity sufficient for execution?).

24 gaps were identified across 4 categories: 8 CRITICAL gaps (require immediate action — primarily tracker status updates and outdated Dashboard metrics that could mislead execution), 7 MODERATE gaps (should be addressed before Year 1 execution begins — primarily cross-reference inconsistencies between early P0 research and final P2–P8 specifications), and 9 MINOR gaps (low-priority refinements that do not affect execution — primarily editorial cross-references and optional structural improvements). NO gaps indicate substandard document quality or missing task coverage. All 391 tasks are substantively addressed in their corresponding documents. The identified gaps are: tracker maintenance issues (not content issues), specification evolution artifacts (early estimates superseded by final decisions), and structural refinements (cross-reference notes and index documents).

2. Gap Summary by Severity

2.1 CRITICAL Gaps (Require Immediate Action)

8 critical gaps identified. All relate to the GTM Master Tracker: P0–P4 task status columns were never updated to “Complete” (despite documents being delivered), and the Dashboard KPI metrics section contains outdated P0-era figures that have been superseded by final product, pricing, and supplier decisions made in P2–P8.

2.2 MODERATE Gaps (Address Before Execution)

7 moderate gaps identified. These involve: cross-reference inconsistencies between early research (P0) and final specifications (P2–P8), a Dashboard manufacturing hub label that should be updated from China/Pakistan to UK/US, and one structural recommendation (Playbook Index document).

2.3 MINOR Gaps (Low-Priority Refinements)

9 minor gaps identified. These are: editorial refinements (cross-reference notes, clarifying language), optional structural improvements (supplier decision memo, folder cover notes), and evolutionary artifacts that are expected in any iterative planning process.

The DOCUMENTS (P6B, P8A) contain the correct final pricing. The TRACKER task descriptions and DASHBOARD KPIs contain the outdated P0-era pricing. Resolution: update the tracker and Dashboard to match the documents.

P3A selects Parkside UK as primary supplier. All P3–P10 documents consistently reference Config B and Parkside. The only inconsistency: the Dashboard still references “China / Pakistan” as the Manufacturing Hub. Resolution: update Dashboard.
